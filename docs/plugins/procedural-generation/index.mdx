# Procedural Generation

<PluginDetails moduleName="NexusProcGen" />

A data-driven system for constructing dynamic, gameplay-focused spaces at runtime. Generation is expressed through a hierarchy of named paradigms that compose to describe a complete space.

## Paradigms

### Cells

Cells are the primary spatial units of a generated space — think rooms, corridors, or distinct gameplay areas. Each Cell defines its bounds and connects to adjacent Cells through **Junctions**.

#### Junctions

Junctions are the transition points between Cells, such as doorways, passages, or portals. They control how Cells relate to one another in the generated layout.

### Organs

Organs populate a Cell's interior — interactive props, cover objects, spawn points, or any content placed within a defined space. They operate within **OrganVolumes** that constrain where and how content can be placed.

#### OrganVolume

An OrganVolume is a bounded region inside a Cell that governs the placement rules for one or more Organs, allowing fine-grained control over content density and positioning.

### Bones

Bones form the underlying structural skeleton of a Cell, providing the rigid anchor points and connective framework that other paradigms are built upon.
