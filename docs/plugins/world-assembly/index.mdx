## Frictionless Technical Elegance

The goal from the start with **World Assembly** was to create a tool that just makes sense for designers and developers alike. This led to the adoption of a _biological nomenclature_ for many of the parts as, for the most part, people are able to understand the idea or pick it up quite quickly.

- You make [Cells](./types/cell.md) (`ULevel`) — a defined gameplay space.
- You add [Junctions](./types/junction-component.md) (`USceneComponent`) to the [Cells](./types/cell.md) — a defined connection point between [Cells](./types/cell.md).
- You build [Tissue](./types/tissue.md) definitions (`UDataAsset`) — A collection of [Cells](./types/cell.md) that can be connected, and the rules governing that.
- You create the [Organ](./types/organ-volume.md)(s) (`AVolume`) — A defined area (or not) you want to assemble, assigning [Tissue](./types/tissue.md)(s) that will contribute.

## Inspiration

From the awe-inspiring interconnected spaces of [Dark Souls](https://store.steampowered.com/app/570940/) to the fast player-responsive generation of [Returnal](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1649240/); the goal is to be able to achieve and exceed both pillars, with one piece of tech. Where you can have an outer [Organ](types/organ-volume.md) defining a forest, with numerous encompassed [Organ](types/organ-volume.md)(s) defining the cities, with individual landmarks inside those cities generating their own interiors, all facilitated and parallelized.
